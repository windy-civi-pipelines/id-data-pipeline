Section 1:
LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 140
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO DAYLIGHT SAVING TIME; PROVIDING LEGISLATIVE INTENT; REPEALING
3 CHAPTER 96, TITLE 67, IDAHO CODE, RELATING TO DAYLIGHT SAVING TIME;
4 AMENDING CHAPTER 1, TITLE 73, IDAHO CODE, BY THE ADDITION OF A NEW SEC-
5 TION 73-123, IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE AN EXEMPTION FROM DAYLIGHT SAVING
6 TIME IN THE MOUNTAIN TIME ZONE PORTION OF IDAHO AND TO PROVIDE THAT THE
7 STATE OF IDAHO SHALL CONFORM TO ANY FEDERAL LAW THAT MAKES DAYLIGHT
8 SAVING TIME PERMANENT; AMENDING CHAPTER 1, TITLE 73, IDAHO CODE, BY THE
9 ADDITION OF A NEW SECTION 73-124, IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE AN EXEMPTION
10 FROM DAYLIGHT SAVING TIME IN THE PACIFIC TIME ZONE PORTION OF IDAHO AND
11 TO PROVIDE THAT THE STATE OF IDAHO SHALL CONFORM TO ANY FEDERAL LAW THAT
12 MAKES DAYLIGHT SAVING TIME PERMANENT; AND PROVIDING CONTINGENCY EFFEC-
13 TIVE DATES.
14 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
15 SECTION 1. LEGISLATIVE INTENT. The Legislature finds and declares
16 that:
17 (1) Changing clocks between Standard Time and Daylight Saving Time is
18 disruptive to health, safety, education, and the economy.
19 (2) Federal law permits observance of only permanent Standard Time by a
20 state exempting itself from Daylight Saving Time as the alternative to bian-
21 nual changing of clocks.
22 (3) Permanent Daylight Saving Time has been repealed in the past due to
23 its harm to health, safety, education, and the economy soon after its trials
24 during World War II and the Oil Crisis of the 1970s.
25 (4) Permanent Standard Time has historically proven to exist without
26 incident for decades.
27 (5) The American Academy of Sleep Medicine, the National Sleep Founda-
28 tion, the Sleep Research Society, and the Society for Research on Biological
29 Rhythms advise against permanent Daylight Saving Time and recommend perma-
30 nent Standard Time.
31 (6) Standard Time is intended to approximate natural solar time.
32 (7) Coordination with contiguous states regarding timekeeping is es-
33 sential to convenience of commerce.
34 SECTION 2. That Chapter 96, Title 67, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
35 hereby repealed.
36 SECTION 3. That Chapter 1, Title 73, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
37 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
38 ignated as Section 73-123, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
39 73-123. EXEMPTION FROM DAYLIGHT SAVING TIME IN THE MOUNTAIN TIME ZONE
40 PORTION. (1) The state of Idaho hereby exempts the portion of the state that
2
1 is in the mountain time zone from the daylight saving time provisions of 15
2 U.S.C. 260a.
3 (2) If the United States congress enacts legislation to mandate per-
4 manent daylight saving time in a majority of all states, the state of Idaho
5 shall conform to such new federal law.
6 SECTION 4. That Chapter 1, Title 73, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
7 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
8 ignated as Section 73-124, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
9 73-124. EXEMPTION FROM DAYLIGHT SAVING TIME IN THE PACIFIC TIME ZONE
10 PORTION. (1) The state of Idaho hereby exempts the portion of the state that
11 is in the Pacific time zone from the daylight saving time provisions of 15
12 U.S.C. 260a.
13 (2) If the United States congress enacts legislation to mandate per-
14 manent daylight saving time in a majority of all states, the state of Idaho
15 shall conform to such new federal law.
16 SECTION 5. Section 3 of this act shall be in full force and effect on and
17 after January 1 of the first year following or coinciding with effectuation
18 of exemption from Daylight Saving Time in the portion of the state of Oregon
19 that is in the Mountain Time Zone. Sections 2 and 4 of this act shall be in
20 full force and effect on and after January 1 of the first year following or
21 coinciding with effectuation of exemption from Daylight Saving Time in the
22 state of Washington.
