Section 1:
LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 181
BY BUSINESS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO CREDIT UNIONS; AMENDING SECTION 26-2105, IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE
3 FOR DELIVERY OF ARTICLES OF INCORPORATION TO THE SECRETARY OF STATE; AND
4 DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 26-2105, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 26-2105. ORGANIZATION. (1) Any seven (7) or more residents of the ju-
9 risdiction of the state of Idaho, of legal age, who have a common bond re-
10 ferred to in section 26-2110, Idaho Code, may organize a credit union and be-
11 come charter members thereof by:
12 (a) Filing an application furnished by the director;
13 (b) Executing articles of incorporation by the terms of which they
14 agree to be bound, which articles shall state:
15 (i) The name, which shall include the words "credit union" and
16 that is not the same as the name of another existing credit union
17 that is authorized to do business in Idaho;
18 (ii) The term of existence of the credit union, which shall be per-
19 petual;
20 (iii) The par value of shares of the credit union, which shall be at
21 least five dollars ($5.00); and
22 (iv) The names and addresses of the subscribers to the articles of
23 incorporation, and the number of shares subscribed by each;
24 (c) Adopting bylaws for the general government of the credit union,
25 consistent with the provisions of this chapter; and
26 (d) Forwarding the required application fee, articles of incorpora-
27 tion, and the bylaws to the director. If they conform to the statute,
28 he shall endorse the articles of incorporation and return two (2) copies
29 of the endorsed articles of incorporation and two (2) copies of the by-
30 laws to the applicants of the credit union, one (1) copy of which is to
31 be for the credit union's permanent files and the other copy to be filed
32 with the county recorder's office in the county in which the principal
33 place of business is located and with the department of finance. The
34 credit union shall deliver one (1) copy of the endorsed articles of in-
35 corporation, together with any required filing fee, to the secretary of
36 state for filing. One (1) original copy of the articles of incorpora-
37 tion and bylaws shall be delivered to and retained by the department of
38 finance. If the director approves or endorses the articles of incor-
39 poration, he will issue three (3) charters in original. The director
40 shall have the authority to investigate the application for charter to
41 determine whether the proposed credit union does meet the objectives of
42 this chapter. The determination for the approval of the application for
2
1 charter shall be under such rules and regulations as shall be adopted
2 by the director. These rules and regulations shall give account to the
3 number of potential members, their stability of employment or member-
4 ship in the group comprising the common bond of membership, and the eco-
5 nomic characteristics of the proposed common bond. If, in the opinion
6 of the director, the proposed credit union does not meet these objec-
7 tives, the charter application shall be denied.
8 (2) The subscribers for a credit union charter shall not transact any
9 business until formal approval of the charter has been received. In order to
10 simplify the organization of credit unions, the director shall cause to be
11 prepared a form of articles of incorporation and a form of bylaws, consis-
12 tent with this chapter, which shall be used by credit union incorporators for
13 their guidance.
14 (3) The articles of incorporation filed in the department of finance
15 shall be available for inspection and a copy may be provided upon payment of
16 an appropriate fee.
17 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
18 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
19 July 1, 2025.
