Section 1:
LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 60
BY HEALTH AND WELFARE COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO EDUCATION; AMENDING CHAPTER 16, TITLE 33, IDAHO CODE, BY THE
3 ADDITION OF A NEW SECTION 33-1637, IDAHO CODE, TO ESTABLISH PROVISIONS
4 REGARDING SUDDEN CARDIAC ARREST PREVENTION; AMENDING SECTION 33-512,
5 IDAHO CODE, TO REQUIRE SCHOOL DISTRICTS TO ESTABLISH CERTAIN POLICIES
6 RELATED TO THE DISCIPLINE OF COACHES; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND
7 PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
8 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
9 SECTION 1. That Chapter 16, Title 33, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
10 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
11 ignated as Section 33-1637, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
12 33-1637. SUDDEN CARDIAC ARREST PREVENTION. (1) For the purposes of
13 this section:
14 (a) "Athletic activity" means an athletic contest, competition, or
15 practice that is sponsored by or associated with a school, including
16 cheerleading, club sponsored sports activities, or sports activities
17 sponsored by any organization affiliated with the school.
18 (b) "Coach" means any employee, contractor, or volunteer responsible
19 for overseeing students participating in any athletic activity.
20 (c) "School" means all public school districts and public charter
21 schools in the state of Idaho.
22 (d) "Sudden cardiac arrest symptoms" means symptoms that include but
23 are not limited to:
24 (i) Fainting;
25 (ii) Seizures during exercise;
26 (iii) Dizziness;
27 (iv) Chest pain;
28 (v) Shortness of breath;
29 (vi) Racing heart rate; or
30 (vii) Unexplained fatigue during exercise or physical activity.
31 (2)(a) The state department of education shall develop and post on
32 its publicly accessible internet-based website guidelines and other
33 relevant materials to inform and educate students participating in,
34 or desiring to participate in, an athletic activity, as well as their
35 parents, guardians, and coaches, about sudden cardiac arrest symptoms,
36 including the risks associated with continuing to play or practice af-
37 ter experiencing sudden cardiac arrest symptoms. Such materials shall
38 include a sudden cardiac arrest symptom information sheet that shall
39 include a list of the sudden cardiac arrest symptoms and an acknowl-
40 edgement to be signed by both a student and such student's parent or
41 guardian. In developing the guidelines and materials, the state de-
42 partment of education may use, at no cost to the state, an educational
2
1 video created by Simon's Heart for the purpose of educating coaches
2 about sudden cardiac arrest.
3 (b) A student participating in, or desiring to participate in, an ath-
4 letic activity and the student's parent or guardian shall, each school
5 year and prior to participation by the student in an athletic activity,
6 sign and return to the student's school an acknowledgment of receipt and
7 review of the sudden cardiac arrest symptoms information sheet provided
8 in paragraph (a) of this subsection.
9 (c) Schools may hold an informational meeting prior to the start of each
10 athletic season for all ages of competitors regarding sudden cardiac
11 arrest symptoms. In addition to students, parents, coaches and other
12 school officials, informational meetings may include physicians, pedi-
13 atric cardiologists, and athletic trainers.
14 (3)(a) A student who passes out or faints while participating in, or
15 immediately following, an athletic activity, or who is known to have
16 passed out or fainted while participating in or immediately following
17 an athletic activity, shall be removed from participation at that time
18 by the coach overseeing the athletic activity.
19 (b) A student who exhibits any of the symptoms provided in subsection
20 (1)(d)(ii) through (vii) of this section during an athletic activity
21 may be removed from participation by a coach if the coach reasonably
22 believes that such symptoms are cardiac related. Coaches who observe
23 such symptoms may notify the student's parent or legal guardian so that
24 such parent or legal guardian can determine what treatment, if any, the
25 student should seek.
26 (c) A student who is removed from play under the provisions of paragraph
27 (a) or (b) of this subsection shall not be permitted to return to par-
28 ticipation in an athletic activity until the student is evaluated and
29 cleared for return to participation in writing by an appropriate medi-
30 cal professional.
31 (4) Every coach shall complete the sudden cardiac arrest training
32 course produced by Simon's Heart and made available through the national
33 federation of state high school associations, or a comparable course pro-
34 duced by another provider and approved by the state department of education,
35 at least once each school year. A coach shall not be eligible to coach an ath-
36 letic activity until the coach completes the training course required under
37 this subsection.
38 (5) Every school shall no later than July 1, 2027, establish school
39 policies that provide the following minimum penalties for a coach found to be
40 in violation of the provisions of subsection (3)(a) or (4) of this section:
41 (a) For a first violation, suspension from coaching any athletic activ-
42 ity for the remainder of the season;
43 (b) For a second violation, suspension from coaching any athletic ac-
44 tivity for the remainder of the season and for the next season; and
45 (c) For a third violation, permanent suspension from coaching any ath-
46 letic activity.
47 (6) No coach shall be subject to civil liability for such coach's ac-
48 tions or inactions under the provisions of this section unless such coach is
49 found to have acted in a grossly negligent or reckless manner.
3
1 (7) The sponsors of youth athletic activities may follow the guidance
2 provided in this section.
3 SECTION 2. That Section 33-512, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
4 amended to read as follows:
5 33-512. GOVERNANCE OF SCHOOLS. The board of trustees of each school
6 district shall have the following powers and duties:
7 (1) To fix the days of the year and the hours of the day when schools
8 shall be in session. However:
9 (a) Each school district shall annually adopt and implement a school
10 calendar that provides its students at each grade level with the follow-
11 ing minimum number of instructional hours:
12 Grades Hours
13 9-12 990
14 4-8 900
15 1-3 810
16 K 450
17 Alternative schools
18 (any grades) 900
19 (b) School assemblies, testing and other instructionally related ac-
20 tivities involving students directly may be included in the required
21 instructional hours.
22 (c) When approved by a local school board, annual instructional hour
23 requirements stated in paragraph (a) of this subsection may be reduced
24 as follows:
25 (i) Up to a total of twenty-two (22) hours to accommodate staff
26 development activities conducted on such days as the local school
27 board deems appropriate.
28 (ii) Up to a total of eleven (11) hours of emergency school clo-
29 sures due to adverse weather conditions and facility failures.
30 However, transportation to and from school, passing times between
31 classes, recess and lunch periods shall not be included.
32 (d) Student and staff activities related to the opening and closing of
33 the school year, grade reporting, program planning, staff meetings, and
34 other classroom and building management activities shall not be counted
35 as instructional time or in the reductions provided in paragraph (c)(i)
36 of this subsection.
37 (e) For multiple shift programs, this rule applies to each shift (i.e.,
38 each student must have access to the minimum annual required hours of
39 instruction).
40 (f) The instructional time requirement for grade 12 students may be re-
41 duced by action of a local school board for an amount of time not to ex-
42 ceed eleven (11) hours of instructional time.
43 (g) The state superintendent of public instruction may grant an ex-
44 emption from the provisions of this section for an individual building
45 within a district, when the closure of that building, for unforeseen
4
1 circumstances, does not affect the attendance of other buildings within
2 the district.
3 (h) The state board of education may grant a waiver of the minimum
4 number of instructional hours for a school district when districtwide
5 school closures are necessary as a result of natural occurrences cre-
6 ating unsafe conditions for students. A county or state disaster
7 declaration must have been issued for one (1) or more of the counties
8 in which the school district is located. A waiver request to the state
9 board of education must describe the efforts by the school district to
10 make up lost instructional hours, the range of grades impacted, and the
11 number of hours the school district is requesting be waived.
12 (i) The reduction of instructional hours allowed in paragraphs (f)
13 through (h) of this subsection may not be combined in a single school
14 year.
15 (2) To adopt and carry on and to provide for the financing of a total ed-
16 ucational program for the district. Such programs in other than elementary
17 school districts may include education programs for out-of-school youth and
18 adults, and such districts may provide classes in kindergarten;
19 (3) To provide, or require pupils to be provided with, suitable text-
20 books and supplies and, for advice on textbook selections, may appoint a cur-
21 ricular materials adoption committee as provided in section 33-512A, Idaho
22 Code;
23 (4) To protect the morals and health of the pupils;
24 (5) To exclude from school children not of school age;
25 (6) To prescribe rules for the disciplining of unruly or insubordinate
26 pupils, including rules on student harassment, intimidation and bullying,
27 such rules to be included in a district discipline code adopted by the board
28 of trustees and a summarized version thereof to be provided in writing at the
29 beginning of each school year to the teachers and students in the district
30 in a manner consistent with the student's age, grade and level of academic
31 achievement;
32 (7) To exclude from school pupils with contagious or infectious dis-
33 eases who are diagnosed or suspected as having a contagious or infectious
34 disease or those who are not immune and have been exposed to a contagious or
35 infectious disease; and to close school if the board determines that condi-
36 tions warrant such closure, based on consultation with the district health
37 department of the public health district in which the school district is lo-
38 cated;
39 (8) To equip and maintain a suitable library or libraries in the school
40 or schools and to exclude therefrom, and from the schools, all books, tracts,
41 papers, and catechisms of sectarian nature;
42 (9) To determine school holidays. Any listing of school holidays shall
43 include not less than the following: New Year's Day, Memorial Day, Indepen-
44 dence Day, Thanksgiving Day, and Christmas Day. Other days listed in section
45 73-108, Idaho Code, if the same shall fall on a school day, shall be observed
46 with appropriate ceremonies; and any days the state board of education may
47 designate, following the proclamation by the governor, shall be school holi-
48 days;
49 (10) To erect and maintain on each schoolhouse or school grounds a suit-
50 able flagstaff or flagpole and display thereon the flag of the United States
5
1 of America on all days, except during inclement weather, when the school is
2 in session; and for each Veterans Day, each school in session shall conduct
3 and observe an appropriate program of at least one (1) class period remember-
4 ing and honoring American veterans;
5 (11) To prohibit entrance to each schoolhouse or school grounds, to pro-
6 hibit loitering in schoolhouses or on school grounds and to provide for the
7 removal from each schoolhouse or school grounds of any individual or indi-
8 viduals who disrupt the educational processes or whose presence is detrimen-
9 tal to the morals, health, safety, academic learning or discipline of the
10 pupils. A person who disrupts the educational process or whose presence is
11 detrimental to the morals, health, safety, academic learning or discipline
12 of the pupils, or who loiters in schoolhouses or on school grounds, is guilty
13 of a misdemeanor;
14 (12) To supervise and regulate, including by contract with established
15 entities, those extracurricular activities that are by definition outside
16 of or in addition to the regular academic courses or curriculum of a public
17 school, and which extracurricular activities shall not be considered to be a
18 property, liberty or contract right of any student, and such extracurricular
19 activities shall not be deemed a necessary element of a public school educa-
20 tion but shall be considered to be a privilege. For the purposes of extracur-
21 ricular activities, any secondary school located in this state that is ac-
22 credited by an organization approved through a process defined by the state
23 department of education shall be able to fully participate in all extracur-
24 ricular activities described in and governed by the provisions of this sub-
25 section;
26 (13) To govern the school district in compliance with state law and
27 rules of the state board of education;
28 (14) To submit to the superintendent of public instruction not later
29 than July 1 of each year documentation that meets the reporting requirements
30 of the federal gun-free schools act of 1994 as contained within the federal
31 improving America's schools act of 1994;
32 (15) To require that all certificated and noncertificated employees
33 hired on or after July 1, 2008, and other individuals who are required by
34 the provisions of section 33-130, Idaho Code, to undergo a criminal history
35 check shall submit a completed ten (10) finger fingerprint card or scan to
36 the department of education no later than five (5) days following the first
37 day of employment or unsupervised contact with students in a K-12 setting,
38 whichever is sooner. Such employees and other individuals shall pay the cost
39 of the criminal history check. If the criminal history check shows that the
40 employee has been convicted of a felony crime enumerated in section 33-1208,
41 Idaho Code, it shall be grounds for immediate termination, dismissal or
42 other personnel action of the district, except that it shall be the right
43 of the school district to evaluate whether an individual convicted of one
44 of these crimes and having been incarcerated for that crime shall be hired.
45 Provided however, that any individual convicted of any felony offense listed
46 in section 33-1208(2), Idaho Code, shall not be hired. For the purposes of
47 criminal history checks, a substitute teacher is any individual who tem-
48 porarily replaces a certificated classroom educator and is paid a substitute
49 teacher wage for one (1) day or more during a school year. A substitute
50 teacher who has undergone a criminal history check at the request of one (1)
6
1 district in which he has been employed as a substitute shall not be required
2 to undergo an additional criminal history check at the request of any other
3 district in which he is employed as a substitute if the teacher has obtained
4 a criminal history check within the previous five (5) years. If the district
5 next employing the substitute still elects to require another criminal his-
6 tory check within the five (5) year period, that district shall pay the cost
7 of the criminal history check or reimburse the substitute teacher for such
8 cost. To remain on the statewide substitute teacher list maintained by the
9 state department of education, the substitute teacher shall undergo a crimi-
10 nal history check every five (5) years;
11 (16) To maintain a safe environment for students by developing a system
12 that cross-checks all contractors or other persons who have irregular con-
13 tact with students against the statewide sex offender registry by developing
14 a school safety plan for each school and by meeting annually with emergency
15 first responders to update the plans and discuss emergency exercises and op-
16 erations;
17 (17) To provide support for teachers in their first two (2) years in the
18 profession in the areas of: administrative and supervisory support, mentor-
19 ing, peer assistance and professional development.
20 (18) To establish policies governing the discipline of coaches pursuant
21 to section 33-1637, Idaho Code.
22 SECTION 3. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
23 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
24 July 1, 2025.
