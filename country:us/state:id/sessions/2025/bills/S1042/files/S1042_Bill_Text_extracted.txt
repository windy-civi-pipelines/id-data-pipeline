Section 1:
LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1042
BY JUDICIARY AND RULES COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO RENTAL APPLICATIONS; PROVIDING LEGISLATIVE INTENT; AMENDING
3 CHAPTER 3, TITLE 55, IDAHO CODE, BY THE ADDITION OF A NEW SECTION 55-315,
4 IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE FOR CERTAIN LIMITATIONS ON RENTAL APPLICATION
5 FEES; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
6 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
7 SECTION 1. LEGISLATIVE INTENT. It is the intent of the Legislature to
8 provide certain limitations on the charging of rental application fees to
9 reduce the number of rental applicants who are charged such fees for rental
10 properties for which they are not considered.
11 SECTION 2. That Chapter 3, Title 55, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
12 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
13 ignated as Section 55-315, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
14 55-315. LIMITATION ON FEES FOR RENTAL APPLICATIONS. (1) A property
15 owner or property manager shall not charge rental application fees to more
16 than two (2) rental applicant households per rental unit concurrently and
17 may charge such fees only if the property owner or property manager:
18 (a) Has a rental unit available for rent or has a rental unit that is
19 reasonably expected to become available for rent within sixty (60)
20 days, unless the rental applicant acknowledges in writing that such
21 rental unit is reasonably expected to become available for rent at a
22 future date beyond sixty (60) days, or unless the rental applicant ac-
23 knowledges in writing that no rental property is available and consents
24 to being placed on a waiting list for future available rental units;
25 (b) Discloses, prior to accepting the rental application, any criteria
26 that the property owner or property manager will review as a condi-
27 tion of accepting the rental applicant as a tenant in the residential
28 unit, including, if applicable, information related to the rental
29 applicant's criminal history, credit score, income, employment or em-
30 ployment history, or rental history; and
31 (c) Completes the criminal history and background check of the ap-
32 plicant for the available rental unit, unless the rental applicant is
33 placed on a waiting list for future available rental units pursuant to
34 paragraph (a) of this subsection.
35 (2) Rental application fees may only be charged to each rental appli-
36 cant within a rental applicant household who requires a criminal history and
37 background check.
38 (3) If the rental application of the first of the two (2) rental ap-
39 plicant households pursuant to subsection (1) of this section is denied, a
40 property owner or property manager may charge a rental application fee to the
41 next rental applicant household and may charge a rental application fee to
2
1 each individual rental applicant of such household who requires a criminal
2 history and background check, as long as no more than two (2) rental appli-
3 cant households are being charged concurrently at any given time.
4 (4) All rental application fees shall conform to industry standards and
5 shall be related to the actual costs of completing a criminal history and
6 background check and any other costs incurred from processing the rental ap-
7 plication.
8 (5) This section shall not apply where a property owner or property man-
9 ager does not charge or receive rental application fees.
10 SECTION 3. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
11 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
12 July 1, 2025.
