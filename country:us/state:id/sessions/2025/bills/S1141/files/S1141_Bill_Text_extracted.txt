Section 1:
LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1141
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO STATE GOVERNMENT; AMENDING CHAPTER 23, TITLE 67, IDAHO CODE, BY
3 THE ADDITION OF A NEW SECTION 67-2341, IDAHO CODE, TO ESTABLISH PROVI-
4 SIONS REGARDING UNAUTHORIZED PUBLIC CAMPING OR SLEEPING, TO DEFINE A
5 TERM, AND TO PROVIDE FOR A CAUSE OF ACTION; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY
6 AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
7 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
8 SECTION 1. That Chapter 23, Title 67, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
9 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
10 ignated as Section 67-2341, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
11 67-2341. STATEWIDE BAN ON UNAUTHORIZED PUBLIC CAMPING OR SLEEP-
12 ING. (1) Notwithstanding any provision of law to the contrary, unauthorized
13 public camping or sleeping pursuant to this section shall be unlawful in
14 the state of Idaho. No political subdivision as defined in section 6-902,
15 Idaho Code, shall authorize, permit, or enable public camping or sleeping on
16 public property, public building grounds, or public roads or rights-of-way
17 within the political subdivision's jurisdiction. The provisions of this
18 section shall not apply to or affect policies, rules, statutes, or leases
19 regarding endowment lands, department of parks and recreation lands, or
20 department of fish and game lands.
21 (2) For purposes of this section, "public camping or sleeping" means
22 unauthorized lodging or residing in a temporary outdoor habitation used as
23 a dwelling, lodging, or living space, which may be evidenced by the erection
24 of a tent or other temporary shelter, including a motor vehicle as defined in
25 section 39-6501, Idaho Code, or a recreational vehicle as defined in section
26 49-119, Idaho Code, and may include the presence of bedding, pillows, cook-
27 ing appliances, heat sources, or the storage of personal belongings. "Pub-
28 lic camping or sleeping" includes sitting, lying, or sleeping on a public
29 walkway creating any hindrance, obstacle, or barrier that interferes with
30 the use of the public walkway.
31 (3) This section does not prohibit or prevent overnight camping for
32 recreational purposes on property designated for such purpose or authorized
33 overnight parking at Idaho rest areas or private businesses in compliance
34 with established rules.
35 (4) Any person or business adversely affected by a violation of this
36 section by a political subdivision shall have a cause of action for injunc-
37 tive relief, damages, and any other relief available under law. A claimant
38 who prevails in an action brought under this section may recover costs and
39 reasonable attorney's fees.
40 (5) The attorney general shall have the power to bring a civil action
41 in any court of competent jurisdiction against any political subdivision to
42 enjoin the political subdivision from violating the provisions of this sec-
2
1 tion. The attorney general may recover reasonable expenses incurred in any
2 civil action brought under this section, including court costs, reasonable
3 attorney's fees, investigative costs, witness fees, and deposition costs.
4 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
5 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
6 July 1, 2025.
