Section 1:
LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION NO. 5
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
2 STATING FINDINGS OF THE LEGISLATURE AND PROVIDING THAT IT IS THE OPINION OF
3 THE IDAHO LEGISLATURE THAT DR. ANTHONY FAUCI'S FEDERAL PARDON DOES NOT
4 CONFER IMMUNITY FROM PROSECUTION OR RESPONSIBILITY FOR STATE CRIMES.
5 Be It Resolved by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 WHEREAS, Dr. Anthony Fauci has served as the Director of the National
7 Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases, helped coordinate the na-
8 tion's response to COVID-19, and served as the Chief Medical Advisor to the
9 President regarding COVID-19; and
10 WHEREAS, on January 20, 2025, former President Joe Biden issued a pre-
11 emptive federal pardon to Dr. Fauci in the waning hours of his administra-
12 tion; and
13 WHEREAS, former President Joe Biden's preemptive federal pardon of Dr.
14 Fauci does not inspire confidence in Dr. Fauci's conduct or the "trust the
15 science" movement promoted by Dr. Fauci; and
16 WHEREAS, it is widely recognized that a federal pardon issued by a pres-
17 ident may absolve an individual of federal offenses but that such a pardon
18 does not extend to any state-level crimes or violations under a state's ju-
19 risdiction; and
20 WHEREAS, Dr. Fauci has not been charged with a crime in the State of
21 Idaho; and
22 WHEREAS, the federal pardon granted to Dr. Fauci would not affect the
23 legal procedures, investigations, or potential prosecution for a state
24 crime under the authority of a state government.
25 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the First Regular Ses-
26 sion of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, the House of Representatives and
27 the Senate concurring therein, that it is the opinion of the Idaho Legis-
28 lature that a federal pardon issued by a president does not confer immunity
29 from prosecution or responsibility for state crimes and that any legal pro-
30 ceedings involving Dr. Fauci should continue to be subject to state law, if
31 applicable.
