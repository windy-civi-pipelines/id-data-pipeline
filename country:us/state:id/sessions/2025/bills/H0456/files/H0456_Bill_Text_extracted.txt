Section 1:
LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 456
BY ANDRUS
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO INDUSTRIAL HEMP; AMENDING SECTION 22-1705, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE
3 A PROVISION REGARDING THE PRODUCTION, PROCESSING, TRANSPORTATION, AND
4 RESEARCH OF INDUSTRIAL HEMP; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING
5 AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
6 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
7 SECTION 1. That Section 22-1705, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
8 amended to read as follows:
9 22-1705. PRODUCTION, PROCESSING, TRANSPORTATION, AND RESEARCH OF IN-
10 DUSTRIAL HEMP AUTHORIZED. (1) Production, processing, transportation, and
11 research of industrial hemp are subject to the rules promulgated under this
12 chapter, the state plan, and the 2018 farm bill and the rules promulgated
13 thereunder.
14 (2) The director must expeditiously promulgate rules that are compli-
15 ant with the 2018 farm bill in time to allow for the production, processing,
16 transportation, and research of industrial hemp in Idaho under the state
17 plan beginning with the spring 2022 growing season of industrial hemp. Any
18 rule formulated and recommended by the Idaho state department of agricul-
19 ture regarding the production, processing, transportation, or research of
20 industrial hemp that is broader in scope or more stringent than federal law
21 or regulations as outlined in the 2018 farm bill or that proposes to regu-
22 late an activity not regulated by the federal government is subject to the
23 following additional requirements: the notice of proposed rulemaking and
24 rulemaking record requirements under chapter 52, title 67, Idaho Code, must
25 clearly specify that the proposed rule, or portions of the proposed rule,
26 are broader in scope or more stringent than federal law or regulations or
27 regulate an activity not regulated by the federal government and delineate
28 which portions of the proposed rule are broader in scope or more stringent
29 than federal law or regulations or regulate an activity not regulated by the
30 federal government.
31 (3) Once a state plan is accepted by the secretary of agriculture, the
32 production, processing, transportation, and research of industrial hemp in
33 Idaho will also be subject to the state plan.
34 (4) The department is authorized to promulgate rules establishing fees
35 and penalties for violations associated with the provisions of this chapter.
36 (5) No penalty may be imposed against a person unless the person was
37 given notice and opportunity for a hearing pursuant to the Idaho administra-
38 tive procedure act, chapter 52, title 67, Idaho Code. A person against whom
39 the department has assessed a penalty under this chapter or the rules pro-
40 mulgated pursuant to this chapter may, within thirty (30) days of the final
41 agency action making the assessment, appeal the assessment to the district
42 court of the county in which the violation is alleged to have occurred.
2
1 (6) Notwithstanding any provision of this chapter:
2 (a) Rules promulgated under this chapter must be adopted through the
3 negotiated rulemaking process; and
4 (b) The transportation of industrial hemp in interstate commerce may
5 continue subject to the provisions of section 67-2921, Idaho Code.
6 (7) Hemp producers do not commit a negligent violation if:
7 (a) The hemp is being produced for the sole purpose of producing grain
8 or fiber;
9 (b) The hemp producer has made reasonable efforts to grow hemp within
10 the acceptable hemp THC level, as defined in agency rule, and in accor-
11 dance with Idaho statutes, rules, and the state hemp plan; and
12 (c) The hemp does not have a total THC, as defined in agency rule, con-
13 centration of more than one percent (1%) on a dry weight basis.
14 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
15 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
16 July 1, 2025.
