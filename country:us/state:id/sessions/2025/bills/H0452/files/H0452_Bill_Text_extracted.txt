Section 1:
LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 452
BY APPROPRIATIONS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO THE APPROPRIATION TO THE PUBLIC SCHOOLS EDUCATIONAL SUPPORT
3 PROGRAM'S DIVISION OF THE IDAHO DIGITAL LEARNING ACADEMY; APPROPRI-
4 ATING ADDITIONAL MONEYS TO THE PUBLIC SCHOOLS EDUCATIONAL SUPPORT
5 PROGRAM'S DIVISION OF THE IDAHO DIGITAL LEARNING ACADEMY FOR FIS-
6 CAL YEAR 2026; APPROPRIATING GENERAL FUND MONEYS FOR TRANSFER TO THE
7 PUBLIC SCHOOL INCOME FUND FOR FISCAL YEAR 2026; CLARIFYING THE ORIG-
8 INAL FUND SOURCE FOR EXPENDITURES IN THE PUBLIC SCHOOLS EDUCATIONAL
9 SUPPORT PROGRAM'S DIVISION OF THE IDAHO DIGITAL LEARNING ACADEMY FOR
10 FISCAL YEAR 2026; RESTRICTING WITHDRAWALS FROM THE PUBLIC EDUCATION
11 STABILIZATION FUND; PROVIDING LEGISLATIVE INTENT ON THE IDAHO DIGITAL
12 LEARNING ACADEMY FUNDING PROCESS AND REQUIRING A REPORT; AMENDING SEC-
13 TION 22 OF HOUSE BILL NO. 251, AS ENACTED BY THE FIRST REGULAR SESSION
14 OF THE SIXTY-EIGHTH IDAHO LEGISLATURE TO REVISE A PROVISION REGARDING
15 TUITION; AMENDING SECTION 33-1020, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE A PROVISION
16 REGARDING FUNDING; PROVIDING FOR CONDITIONS, LIMITATIONS, AND RESTRIC-
17 TIONS; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
18 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
19 SECTION 1. In addition to any other appropriation provided by law,
20 there is hereby appropriated to the Public Schools Educational Support
21 Program's Division of the Idaho Digital Learning Academy an additional
22 $3,577,600 to be expended from the Public School Income Fund for the period
23 July 1, 2025, through June 30, 2026.
24 SECTION 2. There is hereby appropriated and the Office of the State Con-
25 troller shall transfer $3,577,600 from the General Fund to the Public School
26 Income Fund on July 1, 2025, or as soon thereafter as practicable for the pe-
27 riod July 1, 2025, through June 30, 2026.
28 SECTION 3. Of the additional amounts appropriated in Section 1 of this
29 act for the Public Schools Educational Support Program's Division of the
30 Idaho Digital Learning Academy, $3,577,600 shall be considered expended
31 from the General Fund for the period July 1, 2025, through June 30, 2026.
32 SECTION 4. Notwithstanding Section 33-907, Idaho Code, and any other
33 provision of law to the contrary, no moneys shall be withdrawn from the Pub-
34 lic Education Stabilization Fund for the Idaho Digital Learning Academy for
35 services provided during the period July 1, 2024, through June 30, 2025. The
36 Idaho Digital Learning Academy shall rely on existing cash balances for any
37 costs associated with eligible enrollments above the appropriation.
38 SECTION 5. IDAHO DIGITAL LEARNING ACADEMY FUNDING PROCESS. The Second
39 Regular Session of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature shall consider poli-
2
1 cies to verify funds provided by the state for match of individual student
2 enrollment, which may include changing how the state, districts, charters,
3 and public are billed for courses at the Idaho Digital Learning Academy
4 (IDLA). To assist the Legislature, IDLA shall provide a report to the Bud-
5 get and Policy Analysis Division of the Legislative Services Office that
6 details the cost of providing each available service, how IDLA determined
7 the budgeted amount for each service or class, and a detailed report on
8 budgeted and expenditure data for costs associated with operating IDLA. Pro-
9 vided services shall include data on class type, grade level, number of and
10 type of courses enrolled in, and other defining factors. The report shall
11 be provided no later than August 1, 2025, based on data from the 2024-2025
12 school year. The format and content of the report will be determined in con-
13 sultation with the Budget and Policy Analysis Division of the Legislative
14 Services Office.
15 SECTION 6. That Section 22 of House Bill No. 251, as enacted by the
16 First Regular Session of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, be, and the
17 same is hereby amended to read as follows:
18 SECTION 22. IDAHO DIGITAL LEARNING ACADEMY. The Idaho Digital
19 Learning Academy (IDLA), created pursuant to Chapter 55, Title 33,
20 Idaho Code, shall utilize state-appropriated funds for the period
21 July 1, 2025, through June 30, 2026, to achieve the following:
22 (1) Tuition charged by IDLA to Idaho school districts and char-
23 ter schools shall not exceed $75.00 $40.00 per enrollment.
24 (2) Provide remedial coursework for students failing to achieve
25 proficiency in one or more areas of Idaho's standards-based tests.
26 (3) Pursuant to State Board of Education rule, IDAPA 08.02.03,
27 provide advanced opportunities, including access to dual credit
28 courses, for students.
29 The preceding list shall not be construed as excluding other
30 instruction and training that may be provided by the Idaho Digital
31 Learning Academy.
32 SECTION 7. That Section 33-1020, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
33 amended to read as follows:
34 33-1020. IDAHO DIGITAL LEARNING ACADEMY FUNDING. (1) An amount of four
35 hundred thirty dollars ($430) four hundred forty-five dollars ($445) per en-
36 rollment shall be distributed to support the Idaho digital learning acad-
37 emy, created pursuant to chapter 55, title 33, Idaho Code. For the purposes
38 of this section, an "enrollment" shall be counted each time an Idaho school
39 age child enrolls in an Idaho digital learning academy class and continues
40 to be enrolled in such class past the deadline to withdraw, where such with-
41 drawal would not result in the class appearing on such child's transcript in
42 any way. A single child enrolled in multiple classes shall count as multiple
43 enrollments. Summer enrollments shall be included in the fiscal year that
44 begins that summer.
45 (2) The state department of education shall make an estimated distri-
46 bution of funds to the Idaho digital learning academy by no later than July
3
1 31 of each fiscal year, consisting of eighty percent (80%) of the estimated
2 funding for the fiscal year. The balance of all remaining funds to be dis-
3 tributed, pursuant to the calculations in this section, shall be distributed
4 by no later than May 15 of the same fiscal year. The state department of
5 education shall reconcile the enrollments prior to the May 15 payment and
6 withhold or recover any costs necessary to align the funding with verified
7 enrollments. The joint finance-appropriations committee shall consider
8 adjusting the per enrollment amount each legislative session by a percent-
9 age increase equal to the percentage increase approved for salary increases
10 for instructional and pupil service staff pursuant to section 33-1004B,
11 Idaho Code, and for administrative and classified staff pursuant to section
12 33-1004E, Idaho Code.
13 SECTION 8. CONDITIONS, LIMITATIONS, AND RESTRICTIONS. Appropriations
14 made pursuant to this act are subject to the conditions, limitations, and re-
15 strictions specified in this act.
16 SECTION 9. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
17 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
18 July 1, 2025.
