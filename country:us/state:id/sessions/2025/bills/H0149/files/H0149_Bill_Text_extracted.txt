Section 1:
LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 149
BY BUSINESS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO THE IDAHO RESIDENTIAL MORTGAGE PRACTICES ACT; AMENDING CHAPTER
3 31, TITLE 26, IDAHO CODE, BY THE ADDITION OF A NEW SECTION 26-31-211A,
4 IDAHO CODE, TO PROVIDE FOR CONSUMER PRIVACY IN MORTGAGE APPLICATIONS;
5 AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
6 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
7 SECTION 1. That Chapter 31, Title 26, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
8 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
9 ignated as Section 26-31-211A, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
10 26-31-211A. CONSUMER PRIVACY IN MORTGAGE APPLICATIONS. (1) For pur-
11 poses of this section, "mortgage trigger lead" means a consumer report
12 obtained pursuant to section 604(c)(1)(B) of the fair credit reporting act,
13 15 U.S.C. 1681b, where the issuance of the report is triggered by an inquiry
14 made with a consumer reporting agency in response to an application for
15 credit. "Mortgage trigger lead" does not include a consumer report obtained
16 by a lender or servicer that holds or services existing indebtedness of the
17 applicant who is the subject of the report.
18 (2) With regard to a solicitation of a consumer for a residential mort-
19 gage loan, as defined in section 26-31-102, Idaho Code, that is based, in
20 whole or in part, on information contained in a mortgage trigger lead, the
21 following shall be deemed to be a prohibited act or practice:
22 (a) The failure to clearly and conspicuously state in the initial phase
23 of the solicitation that the solicitor is not affiliated with the lender
24 or broker with which the consumer initially applied;
25 (b) The failure to clearly and conspicuously state in the initial phase
26 of the solicitation that the solicitation is based on personal infor-
27 mation about the consumer that was purchased, directly or indirectly,
28 from a consumer reporting agency without the knowledge or permission of
29 the lender or broker with which the consumer initially applied;
30 (c) The failure, in the initial solicitation, to comply with the provi-
31 sions of the federal fair credit reporting act relating to prescreening
32 solicitations that use consumer reports, including the requirement to
33 make a firm offer of credit to the consumer; or
34 (d) Knowingly or negligently using information from a mortgage trigger
35 lead:
36 (i) To solicit consumers who have opted out of prescreened offers
37 of credit under the federal fair credit reporting act; or
38 (ii) To place telephone calls to consumers who have placed their
39 contact information on a federal or state "do-not-call" list.
40 (3) A violation of this section shall constitute a violation of the
41 Idaho consumer protection act.
2
1 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
2 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
3 July 1, 2025.
