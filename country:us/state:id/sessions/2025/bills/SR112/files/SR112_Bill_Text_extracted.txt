LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
IN THE SENATE
SENATE RESOLUTION NO. 112
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 A SENATE RESOLUTION
2 STATING FINDINGS OF THE SENATE, HONORING AND COMMEMORATING THE 250TH AN-
3 NIVERSARY OF PATRICK HENRY'S HISTORIC SPEECH, AND RECOGNIZING THE SIG-
4 NIFICANCE OF PATRICK HENRY'S SPEECH IN THE BIRTH OF AMERICAN LIBERTY.
5 Be It Resolved by the Senate of the State of Idaho:
6 WHEREAS, on March 23, 1775, Patrick Henry delivered one of the most
7 stirring and consequential speeches in American history at the Second Vir-
8 ginia Convention at St. John's Church in Richmond, Virginia; and
9 WHEREAS, in an era of then uncertainty, Henry's words ignited a spirit
10 of resistance and inspired the American colonies toward independence by
11 declaring that the cause of liberty must be pursued with unwavering convic-
12 tion; and
13 WHEREAS, Henry foretold the imminent struggle for American indepen-
14 dence that would commence less than a month later at Lexington and Concord
15 when he proclaimed: "The war is actually begun! The next gale that sweeps
16 from the north will bring to our ears the clash of resounding arms!"; and
17 WHEREAS, Henry exhorted his fellow American patriots, declaring: "I
18 know not what course others may take; but as for me, give me liberty or give
19 me death!"; and
20 WHEREAS, Henry's speech laid a piece of the foundation for American in-
21 dependence, reinforcing the truth that liberty is unalienable and that our
22 rights are not privileges granted by rulers but are inherent endowments from
23 our Creator; and
24 WHEREAS, the legacy of Henry's speech continues to inspire Americans to
25 defend freedom and remain ever vigilant in the protection of constitutional
26 rights, civic engagement, and the principles on which the United States of
27 America was founded; and
28 WHEREAS, in commemorating the 250th anniversary of Henry's historic
29 speech, the Senate reflects on the words, truths, and events that shaped our
30 nation and recommits to the enduring principles of liberty and justice for
31 all.
32 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED by the members of the Senate, assembled
33 in the First Regular Session of the Sixty-eighth Idaho Legislature, that the
34 Senate commemorates the 250th anniversary of Patrick Henry's speech given on
35 March 23, 1775, at the Second Virginia Convention at St. John's Church in
36 Richmond, Virginia, honors its enduring significance in American history,
37 and reaffirms the principles of liberty, civic engagement, and self-gover-
38 nance that Henry so passionately defended. The Senate also recognizes the
39 importance of preserving, teaching, and promoting these ideals and encour-
40 ages the people of Idaho to reflect on the sacrifices made by those who fought
41 for American independence, ensuring that the protection of life, liberty,
42 and the pursuit of happiness is upheld for future generations.
2
1 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that the Secretary of the Senate be, and is hereby
2 authorized and directed to transmit duly certified copies of this resolution
3 to the Governor of Idaho, the Idaho State Historical Society, and the Amer-
4 ica250 in Idaho Advisory Council to further the recognition of this momen-
5 tous occasion.
