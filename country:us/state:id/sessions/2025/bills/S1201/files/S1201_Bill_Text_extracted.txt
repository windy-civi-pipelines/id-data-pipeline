20 and Welfare's Division of Medicaid shall explore a value-based payment model
21 for outpatient addiction treatment based on long-term recovery outcomes.
22 The division shall provide a report on its findings to the Joint Finance-Ap-
23 propriations Committee and the Budget and Policy Analysis Division of the
24 Legislative Services Office no later than January 15, 2026.
25 SECTION 4. MEDICAID CONTRACT PERIODS AND MID-YEAR ADJUSTMENTS. The
26 Department of Health and Welfare's Division of Medicaid shall initiate the
27 process of transitioning multi-year contracts to coincide with the state's
28 fiscal year to enhance transparency. To the extent practicable, the divi-
29 sion shall eliminate mid-year contract adjustments that increase spending
30 without an appropriation from the Legislature. The division shall provide
31 a report on its progress to the Joint Finance-Appropriations Committee and
32 the Budget and Policy Analysis Division of the Legislative Services Office
33 no later than January 15, 2026.
34 SECTION 5. EMERGENCY MEDICAID REPORT. The Department of Health and Wel-
35 fare's Division of Medicaid shall submit an annual report on the number of
36 clients served and the total expenditures reimbursed through the Emergency
37 Medicaid program. The division shall provide the report to the Joint Fi-
38 nance-Appropriations Committee and the Budget and Policy Analysis Division
39 of the Legislative Services Office following the conclusion of each fiscal
40 year, but no later than September 15.
4
1 SECTION 6. FEDERAL FUNDING RESTRICTIONS. Pursuant to Section
2 67-3511(2), Idaho Code, legislative appropriations shall not be transferred
3 from one fund to another fund unless expressly approved by the Legislature.
4 Appropriations made pursuant to this act contingent on anticipated federal
5 funding, grants, or programs shall not be supplanted by state funds in the
6 event of any reduction or elimination of such federal support without leg-
7 islative approval. The agency receiving the appropriation shall take all
8 necessary actions to ensure that the state is not obligated to replace lost
9 federal funds. Pursuant to Section 67-1917, Idaho Code, the agency will
10 submit an inventory of federal funds utilized by the agency. State funds ap-
11 propriated to satisfy match requirements for federal funds shall be reverted
12 to the maximum extent possible if federal funds requiring match are reduced
13 or eliminated. The agency shall promptly notify the Budget and Policy Anal-
14 ysis Division of the Legislative Services Office of any significant changes
15 related to federal funding, grants, or programs. This does not apply to
16 standard federal funding adjustments that take place within an agency's cost
17 allocation plan or indirect expenditure plan for indirect costs and agency
18 support staff, FMAP adjustments, or entitlement programs.
19 SECTION 7. CONDITIONS, LIMITATIONS, AND RESTRICTIONS. Appropriations
20 made pursuant to this act are subject to the conditions, limitations, and re-
21 strictions specified in this act.
