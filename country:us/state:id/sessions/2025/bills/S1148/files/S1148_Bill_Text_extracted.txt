Section 1:
LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1148
BY FINANCE COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO STATE COMMISSIONER SALARIES; AMENDING SECTION 61-215, IDAHO
3 CODE, TO REVISE A PROVISION REGARDING THE SALARY OF PUBLIC UTILITIES
4 COMMISSIONERS; AMENDING SECTION 63-102, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE A PRO-
5 VISION REGARDING THE SALARY OF TAX COMMISSIONERS; AMENDING SECTION
6 72-503, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE A PROVISION REGARDING THE SALARY OF IN-
7 DUSTRIAL COMMISSIONERS; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN
8 EFFECTIVE DATE.
9 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
10 SECTION 1. That Section 61-215, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
11 amended to read as follows:
12 61-215. SALARIES OF PUBLIC UTILITIES COMMISSIONERS. Each member of
13 the public utilities commission shall devote full time to the performance of
14 his/her duties. Commencing on July 1, 2024 2025, the annual salary of mem-
15 bers of the public utilities commission shall be one hundred twenty six thou-
16 sand four hundred twenty-four dollars ($126,424) one hundred twenty-nine
17 thousand six hundred forty-eight dollars ($129,648) and shall be paid from
18 sources set by the legislature.
19 SECTION 2. That Section 63-102, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
20 amended to read as follows:
21 63-102. ORGANIZATION -- CHAIRMAN -- COMPENSATION -- QUORUM -- HEAR-
22 INGS. (1) A member of the state tax commission shall be appointed by the
23 governor, to serve at his pleasure, as chairman. Each member of the state
24 tax commission shall devote full time to the performance of duties. Com-
25 mencing on July 1, 2024 2025, the annual salary for members of the state tax
26 commission shall be one hundred seventeen thousand three hundred ninety-six
27 dollars ($117,396) one hundred twenty thousand six hundred twenty dollars
28 ($120,620).
29 (2) A majority of the state tax commission shall constitute a quorum for
30 the transaction of business. The state tax commission may delegate to any
31 member of the commission or to its employees the power to make investigations
32 and hold hearings at any place it may deem proper and such other matters as
33 will facilitate the operations of the commission.
34 (3) The chairman of the state tax commission shall delegate to each com-
35 missioner the responsibility for policy management and oversight of one (1)
36 or more of the taxes collected and activities supervised or administered by
37 the commission. The state tax commission shall perform the duties imposed
38 upon it by law and shall adopt all rules by majority decision. In any case
39 in which the state tax commission sits as an appellate body upon an appeal
40 from a tax decision from one (1) of the various administrative units subject
2
1 to its supervision, the state tax commissioner charged with responsibility
2 for policy management and oversight of the tax in controversy shall not vote
3 upon the appeal but may advise the remaining members of the commission on the
4 technical aspects of the problems before them.
5 (4) The chairman shall be the chief executive officer and administra-
6 tive head of the state tax commission and shall be responsible for and, with
7 the advice and consent of the state tax commission, may assign responsi-
8 bility for all personnel, budgetary and/or fiscal matters of the state tax
9 commission. Delegations of authority involving personnel, budgetary and/or
10 fiscal matters shall be reviewed by request, and sustained by an affirmative
11 vote, of the majority of the state tax commission.
12 SECTION 3. That Section 72-503, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
13 amended to read as follows:
14 72-503. SALARY. Commencing July 1, 2024 2025, the annual salary of
15 each member of the industrial commission shall be one hundred twenty-three
16 thousand one hundred twenty-six dollars ($123,126) one hundred twenty-six
17 thousand three hundred fifty dollars ($126,350). Industrial commissioner
18 salaries shall be paid from sources set by the legislature. Each member of
19 the industrial commission shall devote full time to the performance of his
20 duties.
21 SECTION 4. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
22 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
23 July 1, 2025.
