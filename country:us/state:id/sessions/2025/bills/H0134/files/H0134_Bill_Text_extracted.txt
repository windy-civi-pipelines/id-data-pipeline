Section 1:
LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 134
BY HEALTH AND WELFARE COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO BREAST CANCER SCREENING; AMENDING TITLE 39, IDAHO CODE, BY THE
3 ADDITION OF A NEW CHAPTER 23, TITLE 39, IDAHO CODE, TO ESTABLISH PROVI-
4 SIONS REGARDING BREAST CANCER SCREENING; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND
5 PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
6 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
7 SECTION 1. That Title 39, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby amended
8 by the addition thereto of a NEW CHAPTER, to be known and designated as Chap-
9 ter 23, Title 39, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
10 CHAPTER 23
11 BREAST CANCER SCREENING
12 39-2301. BREAST CANCER SCREENING. (1) As used in this section, "sup-
13 plemental breast screening" means a medically necessary and clinically
14 appropriate examination of the breast using either standard or abbreviated
15 magnetic resonance imaging, contrast mammogram imaging, or, if such imag-
16 ing is not possible, ultrasound if recommended by the treating physician to
17 screen for breast cancer when there is no abnormality seen or suspected in
18 the breast.
19 (2) This section shall apply only to a health benefit plan that provides
20 benefits for medical or surgical expenses incurred as a result of a health
21 condition, accident, or sickness, including an individual, group, blanket,
22 or franchise insurance policy or insurance agreement, a group hospital ser-
23 vice contract, or an individual group evidence of coverage or similar cover-
24 age document that is offered by:
25 (a) An insurance company;
26 (b) A group hospital service corporation operating pursuant to chapter
27 34, title 41, Idaho Code;
28 (c) A managed care organization operating pursuant to chapter 39, title
29 41, Idaho Code;
30 (d) A fraternal benefit society operating pursuant to chapter 32, title
31 41, Idaho Code; or
32 (e) An exchange operating pursuant to chapter 61, title 41, Idaho Code.
33 (3) Notwithstanding any other provision of law to the contrary, a group
34 or individual health benefit plan providing benefits for medical or surgi-
35 cal expenses incurred as a result of a health condition, accident, or sick-
36 ness shall also provide coverage for breast imaging. The minimum coverage
37 required shall include all costs associated with one (1) supplemental breast
38 screening every year in instances where a person is believed to be at an in-
39 creased risk of breast cancer due to:
40 (a) Personal history of atypical breast histologies;
41 (b) Personal history or family history of breast cancer;
2
1 (c) Genetic predisposition for breast cancer;
2 (d) Prior therapeutic thoracic radiation therapy;
3 (e) Lifetime risk of breast cancer of greater than twenty percent (20%)
4 according to risk assessment tools based on family history;
5 (f) Extremely dense breast tissue based on breast composition cate-
6 gories of the breast imaging and reporting data system established by
7 the American college of radiology; or
8 (g) Heterogeneously dense breast tissue based on breast composition
9 categories with any one (1) of the following risk factors:
10 (i) Personal history of BRCA1 or BRCA2 gene mutations;
11 (ii) First-degree relative with a BRCA1 or BRCA2 gene mutation who
12 has not undergone genetic testing;
13 (iii) Prior therapeutic thoracic radiation therapy from ten (10)
14 to thirty (30) years of age; or
15 (iv) Personal history of Li-Fraumeni syndrome, Cowden syndrome,
16 or Bannayan-Riley-Ruvalcaba syndrome, or a first-degree relative
17 with one (1) of these syndromes.
18 (4) Nothing in this section shall be construed as to prevent the appli-
19 cation of deductible, copayment, or coinsurance provisions contained in the
20 policy or plan for breast imaging in excess of the minimum coverage required.
21 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
22 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
23 January 1, 2026.
