Section 1:
LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 379
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO TRANSPARENT AND ETHICAL GOVERNMENT; AMENDING TITLE 74, IDAHO
3 CODE, BY THE ADDITION OF A NEW CHAPTER 8, TITLE 74, IDAHO CODE, TO PRO-
4 VIDE LEGISLATIVE INTENT AND TO PROVIDE FOR REPORTING OF PAID TRAVEL BY
5 STATE OFFICIALS AND A PENALTY; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING
6 AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
7 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
8 SECTION 1. That Title 74, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby amended
9 by the addition thereto of a NEW CHAPTER, to be known and designated as Chap-
10 ter 8, Title 74, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
11 CHAPTER 8
12 TRANSPARENCY IN TRAVEL BY STATE OFFICIALS
13 74-801. LEGISLATIVE INTENT. It is the intent of the legislature to
14 promote public confidence in government and to promote transparency by re-
15 quiring candidates for statewide offices, statewide elected officials, and
16 officers-elect of statewide offices to report travel paid for by others.
17 74-802. REPORTING OF PAID TRAVEL -- PENALTY. (1) All state officials
18 shall report to the secretary of state all travel outside of Idaho reasonably
19 related to a governmental purpose or to an issue of state, national, or in-
20 ternational public policy paid for by another individual or entity. Travel
21 paid through campaign funds that are reported pursuant to chapter 66, title
22 67, Idaho Code, or solely through personal funds of a state official shall
23 not be subject to the requirements of this section. For the purposes of this
24 section, "state official" means a candidate for statewide office who will
25 appear on a general election ballot, a statewide elected official, and an of-
26 ficer-elect of any statewide office who has prevailed in a general election
27 but has not yet received the oath of office.
28 (2) Within thirty (30) days of the return date of any travel subject
29 to the provisions of subsection (1) of this section, a state official who is
30 subject to the reporting requirements provided for in subsection (1) of this
31 section shall file a report with the secretary of state that includes:
32 (a) The travel destination;
33 (b) The dates of travel;
34 (c) The purpose of the travel; and
35 (d) The name and address of the person or entity that paid for the
36 travel, including whether the travel was a campaign contribution and,
37 in the case of currently serving state officials, whether the travel was
38 made in such state official's capacity as a state official.
39 (3) Any state official who fails to file the report required pursuant
40 to subsection (2) of this section within thirty (30) days shall be subject
2
1 to a late fee in the amount of twenty-five dollars ($25.00) each day until
2 the report is filed, which fee shall be deposited in the public school income
3 fund.
4 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
5 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
6 July 1, 2025.
