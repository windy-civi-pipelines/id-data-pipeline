Section 1:
LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1163
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO TOURISM REVENUE PROTECTION; AMENDING SECTION 63-1801, IDAHO
3 CODE, TO REVISE A SHORT TITLE; AMENDING SECTION 63-1802, IDAHO CODE, TO
4 REVISE PROVISIONS REGARDING LEGISLATIVE INTENT; AMENDING CHAPTER 18,
5 TITLE 63, IDAHO CODE, BY THE ADDITION OF A NEW SECTION 63-1805, IDAHO
6 CODE, TO ESTABLISH PROVISIONS REGARDING PROTECTION OF TOURISM REVENUE
7 AND PROVIDING LIMITATIONS ON THE REGULATION OF SHORT-TERM RENTALS;
8 REPEALING SECTION 67-6539, IDAHO CODE, RELATING TO LIMITATIONS ON THE
9 REGULATION OF SHORT-TERM RENTALS AND VACATION RENTALS; AND DECLARING AN
10 EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
11 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
12 SECTION 1. That Section 63-1801, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
13 amended to read as follows:
14 63-1801. SHORT TITLE. This act chapter shall be known and may be cited
15 as the "Short-term Rental and Vacation Rental Tourism Revenue Protection
16 Act."
17 SECTION 2. That Section 63-1802, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
18 amended to read as follows:
19 63-1802. LEGISLATIVE INTENT. It is the intent of the legislature to
20 ensure that Idaho remains a prime tourism destination and to preserve and
21 enhance income to the state of Idaho and local jurisdictions from tourism.
22 The legislature finds that revenue from tourism is a vital source of income
23 to the state of Idaho and to cities and counties in Idaho and, therefore,
24 must be protected. This act is chapter is also designed to promote access to
25 short-term rentals and vacation rentals by limiting local governmental au-
26 thority to prohibit these beneficial property uses, or to specifically tar-
27 get them for regulation, except in circumstances necessary to safeguard pub-
28 lic health and welfare safety. This act is also Additionally, this chap-
29 ter is designed to preserve and protect constitutionally granted personal
30 property rights and promote property owner access to platforms for offering
31 their properties as short-term rentals and vacation rentals, and enhancing
32 local tax revenue by permitting platforms to assume tax collection and re-
33 mittance responsibilities.
34 SECTION 3. That Chapter 18, Title 63, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
35 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
36 ignated as Section 63-1805, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
37 63-1805. TOURISM REVENUE PROTECTION. (1) No county or city may enact
38 or enforce any ordinance that has the express or practical effect of pro-
39 hibiting any type of short-term rental or vacation rental in the county or
2
1 city. A county or city may implement reasonable regulations as are neces-
2 sary to safeguard public health and safety as long as the reasonable regu-
3 lations do not impose different restrictions or obligations on a short-term
4 rental than are imposed on single family dwellings or similar structures not
5 used as short-term rentals. For the purpose of this section, "different re-
6 strictions or obligations" means any requirement or regulation that would
7 not otherwise apply but for the property's use as a short-term rental. A
8 short-term rental shall be classified as a non-transient residential use for
9 zoning, fire, and building code purposes and shall be subject only to those
10 building codes adopted by the Idaho building code board as such codes apply
11 to other residential uses.
12 (2) All short-term rental properties and owners of such properties
13 shall be subject to all other county and city ordinances and penalties that
14 apply to other residential uses, including but not limited to noise, park-
15 ing, nuisance, curfew, traffic, and other similar regulations.
16 (3) No county or city shall regulate the operation of a short-term
17 rental marketplace as prohibited by the provisions of this chapter.
18 (4) Nothing in this section shall prevent a county or city from requir-
19 ing, by ordinance, a permit to operate a short-term rental as long as the per-
20 mitting ordinance does not impose requirements prohibited by this section.
21 (a) A short-term rental permit shall be issued if a short-term rental
22 owner:
23 (i) Submits payment of an annual fee not to exceed fifty dollars
24 ($50.00) per property; and
25 (ii) Submits a statement certifying that within the twelve (12)
26 month period immediately preceding the application for a permit,
27 there have not been a combined total of three (3) or more convic-
28 tions on three (3) or more separate occasions by the property owner
29 or property manager for violating ordinances relating to such
30 short-term rental property.
31 (b) A county or city may only revoke a permit for a short-term rental
32 property if:
33 (i) A permit holder does not provide payment of the annual permit
34 fee if required by a county or city ordinance adopted pursuant to
35 paragraph (a) of this subsection; or
36 (ii) Within a twelve (12) month period there are a combined total
37 of three (3) or more convictions, on three (3) or more separate oc-
38 casions, by the property owner or property manager for violating
39 ordinances or laws related to such short-term rental property.
40 (5) The owner or operator of a short-term rental shall disclose to
41 prospective tenants prior to renting the short-term rental if the short-term
42 rental is not equipped with:
43 (a) An operating smoke alarm in every room advertised as a sleeping
44 area;
45 (b) An operating fire extinguisher on every floor; and
46 (c) A first aid kit.
47 SECTION 4. That Section 67-6539, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
48 repealed.
3
1 SECTION 5. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
2 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
3 July 1, 2025.
