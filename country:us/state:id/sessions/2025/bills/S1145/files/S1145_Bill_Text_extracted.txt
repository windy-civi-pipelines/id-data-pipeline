Section 1:
LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1145
BY STATE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO EDUCATION; AMENDING CHAPTER 18, TITLE 33, IDAHO CODE, BY THE AD-
3 DITION OF A NEW SECTION 33-1812, IDAHO CODE, TO ESTABLISH PROVISIONS RE-
4 GARDING AN EARLY LITERACY READINESS PROGRAM; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY
5 AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
6 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
7 SECTION 1. That Chapter 18, Title 33, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
8 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
9 ignated as Section 33-1812, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
10 33-1812. EARLY LITERACY READINESS PROGRAM. (1) Each school district
11 and public charter school may establish an early literacy readiness program
12 to ensure that students entering kindergarten are prepared to develop the
13 literacy skills needed for future learning. The program shall support stu-
14 dents in achieving the goal of reading at or above grade level by the end
15 of grade 3 and assist students in achieving growth and proficiency on the
16 statewide literacy assessment.
17 (2) The program shall be available to students four (4) and five (5)
18 years of age who reside within school district boundaries.
19 (3) The program shall be age-appropriate, evidence-based, and aligned
20 with the science of reading. The focus of the program shall be developing
21 foundational reading skills, including, at a minimum, phonemic awareness,
22 phonics, fluency, vocabulary, and text comprehension.
23 (4) The program shall include a parental involvement and engagement
24 component that allows parents to participate in the student's learning at
25 school or at home.
26 (5) School districts and public charter schools may use existing lit-
27 eracy intervention funds received pursuant to section 33-1002(2)(r), Idaho
28 Code. The reporting requirements under section 33-1807(6), Idaho Code,
29 shall apply to this program.
30 (6) The state board of education or its designee shall annually evalu-
31 ate the cost and efficacy of early literacy readiness programs established
32 throughout Idaho pursuant to this section.
33 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
34 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
35 July 1, 2025.
