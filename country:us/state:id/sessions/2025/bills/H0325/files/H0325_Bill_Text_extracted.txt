Section 1:
LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 325
BY WAYS AND MEANS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO THE IDAHO HOUSING AND FINANCE ASSOCIATION; AMENDING SECTION
3 67-6203, IDAHO CODE, TO REMOVE OBSOLETE LANGUAGE; AMENDING SECTION
4 67-6223, IDAHO CODE, TO ESTABLISH A REQUIREMENT REGARDING FEDERAL
5 GRANTS AND TO MAKE TECHNICAL CORRECTIONS; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY
6 AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
7 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
8 SECTION 1. That Section 67-6203, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
9 amended to read as follows:
10 67-6203. COMMISSIONERS -- CHAIRMAN -- APPOINTMENTS. The governor
11 shall appoint seven (7) persons to be commissioners of the Idaho housing
12 and finance association. Preference shall be given to persons representing
13 persons of low income and to persons with experience in the fields of mort-
14 gage lending, finance, banking, real estate, or home building. The governor
15 shall appoint a chairman from among the seven (7) commissioners. The com-
16 missioners shall be appointed for terms of four (4) years, except that all
17 vacancies shall be filled for the unexpired term, and provided that the terms
18 of the first seven (7) commissioners appointed shall end on July 1, 1976, and
19 that the terms of three (3) commissioners next appointed shall end on July
20 1, 1978, and that the terms of the remaining four (4) commissioners so next
21 appointed shall end on July 1, 1980. A commissioner shall hold office until
22 his successor has been appointed and qualifies. A certificate of the ap-
23 pointment or reappointment of any commissioner shall be filed in the office
24 of the secretary of state and in the office of the association, and such cer-
25 tificate shall be conclusive evidence of the due and proper appointment of
26 such commissioner. The governor, the state treasurer, the state controller
27 and the administrator of the division of financial management shall serve as
28 advisors to the commissioners of the association.
29 In addition, two (2) members of the Idaho senate, one (1) from the ma-
30 jority party and one (1) from the minority party, and two (2) members of the
31 Idaho house of representatives, one (1) from the majority party and one (1)
32 from the minority party, shall be appointed by the legislative council to
33 serve as advisors to the commissioners of the association. Such appoint-
34 ments shall be for a term of two (2) years beginning on January 1 of each odd-
35 numbered year, and no appointee shall serve more than two (2) terms. Actual
36 and necessary expenses and per diem shall be allowed as provided for members
37 of the legislative council, and shall be paid from legislative funds. The
38 legislative council shall appoint advisory members as provided herein for
39 terms beginning on July 1, 1980, and expiring January 1, 1981, which terms
40 shall not be included in the prohibition against more than two (2) terms.
2
1 SECTION 2. That Section 67-6223, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
2 amended to read as follows:
3 67-6223. BORROWING POWER -- FINANCIAL ASSISTANCE -- COOPERATION WITH
4 STATE AND FEDERAL GOVERNMENT. In addition to the powers conferred upon the
5 association by other provisions of this chapter, the association is empow-
6 ered to administer any other state, private or federal assistance program
7 including without limitation all tax credit programs, guaranty, loan or
8 investment funds and block grants and, to borrow money or accept contri-
9 butions, grants or other financial assistance or investment from private
10 sources or from the state or federal government for or in aid of any housing
11 project, nonprofit facility, economic development project or agricultural
12 facility within its area of operation, to take over or lease or manage
13 any housing project, nonprofit facility, economic development project or
14 agricultural facility or undertaking constructed or owned by the federal
15 government, and, to these ends, to comply with such conditions and to make
16 such trust indentures, leases or agreements as may be necessary, conve-
17 nient or desirable. It is the purpose and intent of this act to authorize
18 the association to do any and all things necessary or desirable to secure
19 the financial aid or cooperation of the state or federal government in the
20 undertaking, construction, maintenance or operation of any housing project,
21 nonprofit facility, economic development project or agricultural facility
22 by the association. The association is specifically authorized to work
23 with the Idaho department of agriculture in connection with any loan for an
24 agricultural facility and the Idaho department of agriculture shall assist
25 in the provisions of such loans. Provided, however, that any new federal
26 grant that is received by the association on or after July 1, 2026, shall be
27 subject to the provisions of chapter 35, title 67, Idaho Code, including the
28 provisions relating to the expenditure of federal funds and recognition of
29 non-cognizable funds.
30 SECTION 3. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
31 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
32 July 1, 2025.
