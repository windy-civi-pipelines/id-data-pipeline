Section 1:
LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 421
BY APPROPRIATIONS COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO THE CYBERSECURITY AND RESILIENCY FUND; AMENDING CHAPTER 8, TI-
3 TLE 67, IDAHO CODE, BY THE ADDITION OF A NEW SECTION 67-838, IDAHO CODE,
4 TO PROVIDE FOR THE CYBERSECURITY AND RESILIENCY FUND; AND DECLARING AN
5 EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
6 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
7 SECTION 1. That Chapter 8, Title 67, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
8 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
9 ignated as Section 67-838, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
10 67-838. CYBERSECURITY AND RESILIENCY FUND. (1) There is hereby cre-
11 ated in the state treasury the cybersecurity and resiliency fund. The fund
12 shall consist of moneys that may be provided by legislative appropriation or
13 transfer. The state treasurer shall invest the idle moneys of the fund, and
14 the interest earned on such investments shall be retained by the fund.
15 (2) All moneys now or hereafter in the cybersecurity and resiliency
16 fund shall be used to address the state's ongoing needs for information tech-
17 nology infrastructure and cybersecurity technology.
18 (3) The office of information technology services shall create a five
19 (5) year plan for replacement of information technology infrastructure and
20 cybersecurity technology. Every year, the office of information technol-
21 ogy services shall update the plan and submit it to the division of financial
22 management as a part of the annual budget process. The office of information
23 technology services shall also account for and provide information regard-
24 ing any money spent from the fund during the previous fiscal year.
25 (4) For the purposes of this section:
26 (a) "Cybersecurity technology" means technology used to protect net-
27 works, devices, and data from unauthorized access or criminal use and
28 includes network and data center security, endpoint security, cloud se-
29 curity, security information and event management, data protection and
30 recovery, data encryption, identity protection, incident response, se-
31 curity awareness training and audits, penetration testing, technology
32 resilience, and redundancy measures.
33 (b) "Information technology infrastructure" means network infrastruc-
34 ture and computers and storage, including desktops and laptops.
35 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
36 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
37 July 1, 2025.
