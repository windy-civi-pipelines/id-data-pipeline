Section 1:
LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
IN THE HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES
HOUSE BILL NO. 129
BY RESOURCES AND CONSERVATION COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO LAND BANK FUNDS; AMENDING SECTION 58-133, IDAHO CODE, TO EXTEND
3 THE TIME THAT MONEYS CAN BE HELD IN THE LAND BANK FUND; AND DECLARING AN
4 EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
5 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
6 SECTION 1. That Section 58-133, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
7 amended to read as follows:
8 58-133. ACQUISITION, SALE, LEASE, EXCHANGE OR DONATION OF PUBLIC
9 LANDS -- CREATION AND OPERATION OF LAND BANK FUND. (1) The state board of land
10 commissioners may select and purchase, lease, receive by donation, hold in
11 trust, or in any manner acquire for and in the name of the state of Idaho such
12 tracts or leaseholds of land as it shall deem proper, and after inventory
13 and classification as provided herein, shall determine the best use or uses
14 of said lands: provided, however, that all state-owned lands classified
15 as chiefly valuable for forestry, reforestation, recreation and watershed
16 protection are hereby reserved from sale and set aside as state forests.
17 (2) The proceeds from the sale of state endowment land may be deposited
18 into a fund which shall be known as the "land bank fund," which is hereby cre-
19 ated in the state treasury for the purpose of temporarily holding proceeds
20 from land sales pending the purchase of other land for the benefit of the
21 beneficiaries of the endowment. A record shall be maintained showing sep-
22 arately from each of the respective endowments the moneys received from the
23 sale of endowment lands. Moneys from the sale of lands which are a part of an
24 endowment land grant shall be used only to purchase land for the same endow-
25 ment.
26 (3) All moneys deposited in the land bank fund, including earnings
27 on those moneys, are hereby continually appropriated to the state board of
28 land commissioners for the purposes enumerated in this section. The state
29 board of land commissioners may hold proceeds from the sale of land in the
30 land bank fund for a period not to exceed five (5) ten (10) years from the
31 effective date of sale. If, by the end of the fifth tenth year, the proceeds
32 from the land sale have not been encumbered to purchase other land within the
33 state, the proceeds shall be deposited in the permanent endowment fund of the
34 respective endowment along with any earnings on the proceeds from the land
35 sale, unless the period is extended by the legislature.
36 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
37 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
38 July 1, 2025.
