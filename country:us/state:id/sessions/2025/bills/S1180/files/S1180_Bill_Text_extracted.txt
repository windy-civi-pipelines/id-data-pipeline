Section 1:
LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1180
BY JUDICIARY AND RULES COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO TRAFFIC ENFORCEMENT; AMENDING CHAPTER 14, TITLE 49, IDAHO CODE,
3 BY THE ADDITION OF A NEW SECTION 49-1432, IDAHO CODE, TO ESTABLISH PRO-
4 VISIONS REGARDING AUTOMATED LICENSE PLATE READERS AND DATA; AMENDING
5 SECTION 49-428, IDAHO CODE, TO REVISE A PROVISION REGARDING DISPLAY OF
6 PLATE AND STICKERS; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING AN EFFEC-
7 TIVE DATE.
8 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
9 SECTION 1. That Chapter 14, Title 49, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
10 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
11 ignated as Section 49-1432, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
12 49-1432. AUTOMATED LICENSE PLATE READERS -- DATA ACCESS AND CON-
13 TROL. (1) For the purposes of this section:
14 (a) "Agency" means the state of Idaho, any political subdivision of the
15 state of Idaho, or any agency thereof.
16 (b) "Automated license plate reader" means a device that passively
17 records license plates of vehicles on a roadway or any similar device.
18 (c) "Traffic flow analysis" means the study and real-time use of data
19 regarding vehicles moving through a transportation network in order to
20 improve traffic flow, reduce congestion, and increase safety.
21 (2) An agency may use an automated license plate reader and its data
22 for law enforcement purposes, traffic flow analysis purposes, or both. An
23 agency that uses an automated license plate reader for law enforcement pur-
24 poses shall do so pursuant to the following requirements:
25 (a) The device and its data shall not be used for any purpose other than
26 the investigation of felony or misdemeanor crimes or traffic accidents
27 or to search for missing or endangered persons; and
28 (b) The device and its data shall not be used to enforce traffic infrac-
29 tions other than those associated with a traffic accident.
30 (3) Access to automated license plate reader data shall be limited to an
31 agency's authorized personnel. Agencies shall implement access controls to
32 ensure that only individuals with appropriate clearance are able to retrieve
33 automated license plate reader data.
34 (4) Except as expressly allowed pursuant to this section, automated
35 license plate reader data shall not be used for any non-law enforcement
36 purpose, including but not limited to personal use, political activity, or
37 surveillance unrelated to the purposes described in subsection (2)(a) of
38 this section.
39 (5) A person who misuses automated license plate reader data pursuant
40 to subsection (4) of this section shall be subject to discipline, adminis-
41 trative penalties, and criminal prosecution, if applicable.
2
1 (6) Each agency that uses automated license plate readers shall conduct
2 mandatory training for all authorized users regarding data privacy, legal
3 compliance, and ethical use of automated license plate reader systems.
4 (7) All access to and queries of automated license plate reader data
5 shall be logged in a secure audit system to include user identity, date,
6 time, and the purpose of access.
7 (8) Agencies shall conduct semiannual audits of automated license
8 plate reader data access logs to detect and address any unauthorized or sus-
9 picious activity.
10 SECTION 2. That Section 49-428, Idaho Code, be, and the same is hereby
11 amended to read as follows:
12 49-428. DISPLAY OF PLATE AND STICKERS. (1) License plates assigned to
13 a motor vehicle shall be attached, one (1) in the front, if the vehicle is
14 equipped with a front license plate mounting bracket, and the other in the
15 rear, with the exception of the following:
16 (a) The license plate assigned to a motorcycle, all-terrain vehicle,
17 utility type vehicle, motorbike or semitrailer and the license plate
18 assigned to a motor vehicle operated by a manufacturer, repossession
19 agent or dealer shall be attached to the rear.
20 (b) Vehicles displaying year of manufacture, old timer, classic car,
21 street rod, or custom vehicle license plates shall be allowed to display
22 one (1) plate attached to the rear of the vehicle.
23 (c) The license plate attached to a tractor shall be attached to the
24 front.
25 (d) The wrecker plate shall be displayed on the vehicle being towed in
26 such a manner as to be visible when the vehicle being towed is approached
27 from the rear.
28 License plates shall be displayed during the current registration year. The
29 annual registration sticker for the current registration year shall be dis-
30 played on each license plate, except for trailers, semitrailers, and commer-
31 cial vehicles over twenty-six thousand (26,000) pounds under the provisions
32 of sections 49-434 and 49-435, Idaho Code. For the purposes of this title,
33 the license plates together with the registration stickers shall be consid-
34 ered as license plates for the year designated on the registration sticker.
35 For purposes of this chapter, a validation sticker issued to the operator of
36 an all-terrain vehicle, utility type vehicle, or motorbike under the provi-
37 sions of sections 67-7122 and 67-7124, Idaho Code, shall be considered the
38 registration sticker.
39 (2) Every license plate shall at all times be securely fastened to the
40 vehicle to which it is assigned to prevent the plate from swinging, be at a
41 height not less than twelve (12) inches from the ground, measuring from the
42 bottom of the plate, be in a place and position to be clearly visible, and be
43 maintained free from foreign materials and in a condition to be clearly legi-
44 ble, and all registration stickers shall be securely attached to the license
45 plates and shall be displayed as provided in section 49-443(4), Idaho Code.
46 SECTION 3. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
47 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
48 July 1, 2025.
