Section 1:
LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF IDAHO
IN THE SENATE
SENATE BILL NO. 1152
BY JUDICIARY AND RULES COMMITTEE
1 AN ACT
2 RELATING TO JURISDICTION ON MILITARY INSTALLATIONS; AMENDING CHAPTER 7, TI-
3 TLE 58, IDAHO CODE, BY THE ADDITION OF A NEW SECTION 58-708, IDAHO CODE,
4 TO ESTABLISH PROVISIONS REGARDING JURISDICTION OVER CERTAIN INDIVIDU-
5 ALS ON MILITARY INSTALLATIONS; AND DECLARING AN EMERGENCY AND PROVIDING
6 AN EFFECTIVE DATE.
7 Be It Enacted by the Legislature of the State of Idaho:
8 SECTION 1. That Chapter 7, Title 58, Idaho Code, be, and the same is
9 hereby amended by the addition thereto of a NEW SECTION, to be known and des-
10 ignated as Section 58-708, Idaho Code, and to read as follows:
11 58-708. JURISDICTION OVER CIVILIANS ON MILITARY INSTALLATIONS. (1)
12 Notwithstanding any current jurisdiction authorized by existing state or
13 federal statute, the state of Idaho hereby accepts the transfer of juris-
14 diction over adult and juvenile civilian offenders from the United States
15 on military lands or portions of military lands upon request from the United
16 States and delegates and directs the governor to ensure the legal completion
17 of such requests.
18 (2) Any person designated by the federal government may file a re-
19 quest with the governor for change in jurisdiction over adult and juvenile
20 civilian offenders on certain property or portions of a property held by the
21 United States and operated for military purposes, and the governor is hereby
22 authorized to accept such a request, ensuring adequate prosecution and ac-
23 cess to justice for adult and juvenile civilian offenders and protection for
24 victims of such offenders for offenses that occur on military lands in Idaho.
25 (3) A legally adequate request shall contain:
26 (a) A description of the property or portion of the property over which
27 the transfer of jurisdiction for adult and juvenile civilian offenders
28 is sought, described in terms legally sufficient to identify the bound-
29 aries of the property;
30 (b) A description of the existing jurisdiction over the property held
31 by the United States;
32 (c) A description of the jurisdiction over adult and juvenile civilian
33 offenders that the United States proposes to be transferred; and
34 (d) A statement by the requesting person that the person has the author-
35 ity to make the request on behalf of the United States.
36 (4) The transfer of jurisdiction shall not be exclusive jurisdiction,
37 shall not affect any other existing jurisdiction other than granting Idaho
38 courts jurisdiction, and shall not affect any other jurisdiction agree-
39 ments. The details of coordination efforts may be further negotiated in a
40 memorandum of understanding or law enforcement cooperation agreement be-
41 tween the United States military service operating the military lands and
42 the respective state or local law enforcement agency.
2
1 (5) The transfer of jurisdiction over adult and juvenile civilian of-
2 fenders shall become effective upon:
3 (a) The governor's filing of the documents signifying such request and
4 acceptance with the office of the Idaho secretary of state; and
5 (b) The governor's filing of the documents signifying such request and
6 acceptance with the applicable local offices of county land records.
7 (6) No transfer of jurisdiction authorized pursuant to this section
8 shall in any way restrict the state of Idaho's sole determination regarding
9 when an adult or juvenile civilian offender shall or shall not be prosecuted,
10 including a request to transfer a juvenile offender to adult court by the
11 appropriate prosecuting authority.
12 SECTION 2. An emergency existing therefor, which emergency is hereby
13 declared to exist, this act shall be in full force and effect on and after
14 July 1, 2025.
